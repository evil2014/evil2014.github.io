Figure one shows the I.P.V.6. routing table for router R-1 produced from the command, show i.p. route.  The figure highlights the three remote networks learned through E.I.G.R.P.  Figure two lists one of the entries and identifies each piece of the entry.  The line reads D for the route source being E.I.G.R.P., 2 0 0 1 colon D B 8 colon C A F E colon 3 colon colon slash 64 for the network address, 90 for the administrative distance, slash 2 1 7 0 1 1 2 for the metric.  The line ends with via F E 8 0 colon colon 3 for the next hop I.P. address and serial zero slash zero slash zero for the outgoing interface.
