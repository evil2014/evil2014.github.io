Figure one shows the output from the command-line utility netstat.  In this figure the first column is highlighted and is listing the protocol in use during the current connection to the local machine, in the example it is T.C.P. Figure two shows the second column and lists the port used for the current connections, and in the example port 3166 has a current connection running. Figure three shows the third column, which lists the foreign address used for the connection, and in the example the foreign address is www.cisco.com. Figure four shows the destination port, which is part of the third column in the output, the destination address.  In the example, it is listed as H.T.T.P. Figure five shows the fourth column in the netstat output, which is the state of the connection.  In the example the connection is in the established state.
